Problem set 1

  1. The overall prevalence of HIV is 2% of the population. The HIV diagnostic exam has sensitivity (probability of detecting HIV given that the person is infected) of 0.98 and specificity (probability of giving a negative result given that the person is not infected) of 0.95. What is the probability that the person is infected, given a positive test result? Based on your previous answer, should HIV testing be required for everyone?

  2. This example was taken from (Agresti 2002). For this exercise, assume that these data were collected from a prospective study (i.e., not a case-control study in which individuals were purposefully sampled according to their cancer status). Does smoking cause lung cancer? Use the data on the table below together with conditional probabilities to answer this question. What could be potential problems with your analysis? Can we definitively conclude that smoking causes lung cancer?

Smoker Lung cancer cases Controls
Yes 688 650
No 21 59

These are data from Doll and Hill, 1950. British Medical Journal. This table shows individuals cross-classified according to their smoking status and if they had lung cancer or not.
